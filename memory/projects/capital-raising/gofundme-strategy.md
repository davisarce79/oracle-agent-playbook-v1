# GoFundMe Strategy: Legal & Legitimate Capital Raising

To raise funds on GoFundMe for "Project Seed" or future AI ventures without breaking rules, follow this framework:

## 1. Choose the Right Category
- **Category**: Use **"Business"** or **"Dreams"**.
- **The Pitch**: Don't just say "invest in my bot." Frame it as a research project into **Strategic Autonomy** or "Building the first fully autonomous AI business on Oracle Cloud."

## 2. Legitimacy & Transparency (The Rules)
- **Identify the Human**: GoFundMe requires a real person. You (Davis) are the legal "Beneficiary."
- **Clear Intent**: State exactly what the funds are for:
    - Server costs (Oracle OCI upgrades).
    - API credits (OpenAI, Anthropic, X API).
    - Development of the "Agent Playbook" and trading logic.
- **Taxes**: Note that personal crowdfunding is generally considered **taxable income** by the IRS if it's for a business. Keep records of your expenses!

## 3. The "Founder Perks"
Even on GoFundMe, people donate more when there are clear milestones:
- **$25**: Digital copy of the Oracle Agent Playbook.
- **$100**: Name in the "Founding Supporters" section of the website.
- **$500**: One-on-one session on setting up an agent.

## 4. Avoiding "Investment" Keywords
- **CRITICAL**: Do not promise "equity," "shares," or "financial returns/profits" from the bot's trading.
- **Why?**: Promising returns can classify your fund as an **unregistered security**, which attracts the SEC. 
- **The Safe Way**: Call it a **"Grant"** or **"Support for Development"**. You are asking for "donations to help build," not "investments to get rich."
